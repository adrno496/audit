Hero section: 8 rules to convert from the first second

Above the fold, you play 80% of your conversion. Here are 8 timeless rules, illustrated with instructive counter-examples.

May 5, 20261,000 words

"Above the fold" refers to the area visible without scrolling. ~800 px on desktop, ~600 px on mobile.

Rule 1 — Answer "What & for whom?" in 5 seconds

Naive visitor test: show the page for 5 seconds, hide it, ask: "What does it do, and for whom?". If they hesitate, your hero failed.

Good: "Schedule 30 days of Instagram posts in 10 minutes. For solo community managers."
Bad: "The next-generation platform for community managers."

Rule 2 — Sub clarifies, doesn't repeat

Sub specifies the mechanism or secondary benefit. If you can delete it without losing info, it's redundant.

Rule 3 — One primary CTA, visible without scrolling

Colored button, large size, action+benefit text. Not "Submit", not "Learn more".

Rule 4 — Visual reinforces, doesn't distract

  • Product mockup: ideal for SaaS
  • Real photo of target user: emotional, services and training
  • (Muted) video: 15-30s autoplay, visual products

Avoid: generic stock photo of diverse smiling team in front of MacBook.

Rule 5 — Immediate trust strip

Just under the hero, add a mini-strip: customer logos, average rating, user count.

"Used by 12,000 freelancers · 4.8/5 on Trustpilot"

Rule 6 — Max 3 competing visual elements

A hero with headline + sub + 2 CTAs + image + testimonial + logos + promo + countdown is too much. Max 3 primary elements.

Rule 7 — Mobile-first, always

Over 60% of traffic is mobile. Design for mobile first. If it works at 375 px wide, it'll work on desktop.

Rule 8 — Test and iterate

Hero is the priority for A/B tests. Test: headline, CTA wording, visual type, trust strip presence. One variable per test, 2-4 weeks minimum.

Recap

  1. Answer "what & for whom" in 5s
  2. Sub clarifies, doesn't repeat
  3. One primary CTA visible without scrolling
  4. Visual that reinforces, not distracts
  5. Immediate trust strip
  6. Max 3 competing visual elements
  7. Mobile-first, always
  8. Test and iterate
