Social proof: 7 formats to reassure without overdoing it

Testimonials, customer logos, counters, certifications, case studies. What still works in 2026, what smells fake, and how to dose to gain trust without losing credibility.

April 29, 2026900 words

Social proof is one of the most powerful conversion levers. But wrong dosage costs cash: too much and you smell desperate; too little and you seem clientless.

1. Testimonials with photo, name, role, company

The king format. The 4 elements must be together. Ideal quantity: 3-6.

Trap: "This product changed my life! — Marie" with generic photo. Detected from 100 m.

2. Recognizable customer logos

Powerful if you have real significant logos. Max 6-8. Grayscale to stay sober.

Classic trap: 10-person startup showing Apple, Google, Microsoft.

3. Concrete counters and numbers

  • Number must be impressive for the audience
  • Number must be plausible
  • Starting? Use "4.8/5 from 23 reviews"

4. Verified ratings (Trustpilot, G2, Capterra)

Badges from a third party = max credibility. Link directly to the listing.

5. Case studies with quantified results

Premium for B2B. Photo + name + role, context, quantified results, citation, link to full study.

6. Technical certifications (B2B)

SOC 2, ISO 27001, GDPR, AWS Advanced. Discreet at the bottom or in a "Security & compliance" strip.

7. Press mentions

Works if cited media are recognized. Otherwise show nothing.

The dosage

  1. Hero strip: rating + user count
  2. Below hero: 4-6 logos
  3. Benefits section: 1 testimonial per benefit
  4. Dedicated section: 3-6 full format
  5. Case studies (B2B): 2-3
  6. Footer: badges

Traps to avoid

  • Anonymous testimonials
  • Stock photos
  • More than 6 in a row
  • Empty quotes ("This product is great")
  • Vague counters ("Thousands of users")

Special case: starting, no clients

  • 10-20 free beta-testers → testimonials
  • Personal credibility ("Ex-Google, 8 years B2B growth")
  • Method and expertise (blog, podcasts, talks)
  • Honesty: "We just launched, here's why" beats fake social proof
