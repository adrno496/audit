How to audit a landing page in 2026: the complete method in 8 steps

The framework we use in ConvertAudit, explained step by step. Weighting, dimensions, traps, concrete examples: the reference guide.

May 10, 20261,500 words

Why audit (and not just look)?

Most landing pages fail on basic mistakes. Not on subtle wording — on mistakes an expert eye spots in under ten seconds.

The problem: these mistakes are invisible to their author. When you look at your own page, you see your product, your intent. Visitors see a wall of text and no reason to trust.

Prep your audit in 5 minutes

  1. Open the page in private browsing on mobile AND desktop.
  2. Define the single objective: purchase, booking, email signup?
  3. Identify the audience: overworked SaaS decision-maker, hesitant e-commerce shopper?

Step 1 — Evaluate the Hero (×2)

The Hero zone drives up to 80% of the decision. Weight ×2.

  • Headline answers "What & for whom?" in 5 seconds?
  • Sub clarifies the main benefit?
  • CTA visible above the fold?
  • Visual reinforces or distracts?

Step 2 — Diagnose copywriting (×1.5)

Structure

Does the page follow a canvas (AIDA, PAS)? Without narrative structure, visitors don't figure it out.

Benefits / features ratio

"Stores 1 TB" is a feature. "Never lose a file" is a benefit. Aim for 70/30.

Readability

Short sentences (max 20 words), 2-4 line paragraphs, no empty buzzwords.

Step 3 — Audit CTAs (×2)

  • How many primary CTAs? Ideally 1.
  • Visible at every major scroll?
  • Action-and-benefit oriented wording?
  • Strong contrast?
Classic mistake: 4 competing CTAs of equal visual weight. Visitor freezes.

Step 4 — Social proof

  • 2-5 testimonials with photo, name, role, company
  • Recognizable customer logos
  • Concrete numbers: "12,000 audits completed"
  • Case studies with quantified results (B2B)

Step 5 — Visual structure

  • Consistent H1 → H2 → H3 hierarchy
  • Logical sections, generous spacing
  • No text wall longer than 5 lines

Step 6 — Trust signals

  • Visible HTTPS
  • Legal notice and privacy policy in footer
  • Guarantee / refund policy
  • FAQ anticipating objections

Step 7 — Evaluate urgency

Honest urgency — not fake countdowns — is a powerful lever: "Enrollment open until June 30", "5 spots left".

Caution: detected fake urgency destroys trust.

Step 8 — Test on mobile

  • Renders correctly at 375 px wide
  • Thumb-accessible CTAs
  • Working forms (autocomplete, proper input types)
  • < 3s load on 4G
