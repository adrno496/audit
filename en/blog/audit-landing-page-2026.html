How to audit a landing page in 2026: the complete 8-step method

The framework we use in ConvertAudit, explained step by step. Weighting, dimensions, traps, concrete examples: the reference guide for self-auditing or auditing a client.

Why audit (and not just "look")?

Most landing pages fail on basic mistakes. Not subtle wording issues or fine behavioral psychology — mistakes an expert eye spots in under ten seconds.

The problem: these mistakes are invisible to their author. When you look at your own page, you see your product, your intent, your mental journey. Visitors see a wall of text, buttons everywhere, and no reason to trust.

Auditing means casting a structured, cold look at the page, following a grid that forces you to see what's actually there.

Prep your audit in 5 minutes

Before scoring anything, do these three things:

  1. Open the page in private browsing on both mobile and desktop. Clear your cache. You should see the page like a new visitor.
  2. Define the single objective: purchase? booking? email signup? download? One priority action — the audit scores everything against it.
  3. Identify the target audience: overworked SaaS decision-maker, hesitant e-commerce shopper, freelance tool-shopper? Tone, text length and proof depend on this.

Without these three, your audit scores in a vacuum. Every performant landing page answers a precise job-to-be-done for a precise audience.

Step 1 — Evaluate the Hero (×2)

The Hero zone (above the fold, what shows without scrolling) drives up to 80% of the stay-or-leave decision. It's the most-weighted dimension in our framework: ×2.

Ask:

  • Does the headline answer "What is it and for whom?" in under 5 seconds?
  • Does the sub-headline clarify the main benefit without jargon?
  • Is there a CTA visible above the fold?
  • Does the visual (mockup, video, illustration) reinforce the message or distract from it?

Score 9-10/10: a naive visitor understands the product and wants to click within 5 seconds. Score 0-3: they have to scroll, read, deduce.

Step 2 — Diagnose copywriting (×1.5)

Copywriting is the second most-weighted dimension. We score structure, benefits/features ratio, and readability.

Structure

Does the page follow a proven canvas (AIDA: Attention-Interest-Desire-Action; or PAS: Problem-Agitation-Solution)? A page without narrative structure leaves visitors to figure it out themselves. They don't.

Benefits/features ratio

A good page sells outcomes, not features. "Stores 1 TB" is a feature. "Never lose an important file again" is a benefit. Count both on your page: for a non-technical prospect, aim for 70% benefits / 30% features.

Readability

Short sentences (max 20 words), 2-4 line paragraphs, simple words. Ban the empty buzzwords ("innovative solution", "next-generation platform"). Flesch-Kincaid score below 60 = too technical for general audience.

Step 3 — Audit CTAs (×2)

The call-to-action is the second double-weighted dimension. Key questions:

  • How many primary CTAs? Ideally 1 (not 5).
  • Visible at every major scroll? Visitors should never have to search.
  • Action and benefit-oriented wording? "Start my free trial" beats "Submit" hands down.
  • High-contrast color against the background?
  • Perceived friction? "Request a quote" is more engaging than "Buy now — $999" for some profiles.
Classic mistake: 4 competing CTAs with equivalent colors and sizes. Visitor doesn't know which is the button to click. Hierarchize: one primary CTA, less prominent secondaries.

Step 4 — Measure social proof (×1)

No testimonials? You signal "no one uses me." Too many stereotypical ones? You signal "I made up my customers." The right dose:

  • 2-5 testimonials with photo, name, role, company (all four together — otherwise it smells fake)
  • Recognizable customer logos in the Hero zone ("used by X, Y, Z")
  • Concrete numbers: "12,000 audits completed", "4.8/5 from 200 reviews"
  • Case studies with quantified results for B2B SaaS

Read our dedicated guide: Social proof: 7 formats to reassure without overdoing it.

Step 5 — Check visual structure (×1)

A well-structured page scans before reading. Indicators:

  • Consistent H1 → H2 → H3 hierarchy (one H1 only)
  • Logical sections with generous spacing
  • No text wall longer than 5 lines
  • Bullet points and numbered lists for enumerations

Step 6 — Spot trust signals (×1)

Especially for purchases and signups:

  • Visible HTTPS (lock icon)
  • Footer link to legal notice and privacy policy
  • Explicit guarantee or refund policy
  • FAQ that anticipates common objections
  • Recognized payment / partner logos

Step 7 — Evaluate urgency (×1)

Honest urgency — not the fake countdown that resets each visit — is a powerful lever: "Enrollment open until June 30", "5 spots left in June", "Launch promo -30%, until Sunday".

Caution: fake urgency detected by savvy visitors destroys trust. If you put a countdown, make it real.

Step 8 — Test on mobile and perf (×1)

Over 60% of web traffic is mobile. Verify:

  • Page renders correctly at 375px wide
  • CTAs are thumb-accessible, not too small
  • Forms work (autocomplete, proper input types)
  • Loading under 3s on 4G
  • No cookie banner blocking all content

Synthesis: your score out of 100

Once each of the 8 dimensions scored out of 10, apply this weighting:

DimensionWeight
Hero×2
CTA×2
Copywriting×1.5
Social proof×1

Trust×1
Urgency×1
Mobile & perf×1

Theoretical max: 95 points. Scale to 100. Mention: < 40 Weak, 40-60 Average, 60-80 Good, > 80 Excellent.
