15 critical mistakes on SaaS landing pages

The most frequent frictions seen on 100+ SaaS pages. For each: why it's a problem and the concrete fix.

May 8, 20261,200 words

Before writing this guide, we reviewed 100+ SaaS landings. The same mistakes recur. Here are the 15 most expensive ones, by frequency.

1. The generic headline that sells nothing

"The next-generation platform to boost productivity". Could sell anything.

Fix: name the job-to-be-done. "Schedule 30 days of Instagram posts in 10 minutes".

2. No CTA above the fold

Fix: a primary CTA visible without scrolling, on desktop and mobile.

3. Three competing CTAs of equal visual weight

Fix: one primary CTA, sober secondary CTAs.

4. Pricing hidden behind "Request a quote"

70% of decision-makers want to see the price before booking a call.

Fix: at least show a starting point. "From $49/month".

5. Demo that demands email + name + company + phone before showing the product

Fix: embed a GIF / video / mini-demo. Form comes after value is demonstrated.

6. Blurry, outdated screenshots

Fix: HD recent screenshots, realistic data, mocked in stylized browser.

7. Too many customer logos without context

"Apple, Google, Microsoft" on a 10-person startup. Nobody believes it.

Fix: 4 real relevant logos beat 12 dubious ones.

8. Stereotypical testimonials without faces

Fix: name, role, company, real photo. Ideally with verifiable LinkedIn link.

9. No price on the homepage

Fix: "Pricing" link in main menu, accessible in under 2 clicks.

10. Cookie banner covering everything on mobile

Fix: compact banner, clear "Accept" and "Reject" of equal visual weight.

11. Empty FAQ or wrong objections

Fix: list the 5-7 real objections you hear in demos. Answer frankly.

12. No security signals (B2B)

Fix: "Security & compliance" strip with applicable certifications.

13. Hasty competitor comparison

Fix: be honest about what the competitor does better.

14. Auto-play video with sound on Hero

Fix: muted autoplay. Sound only on user action.

15. 8,000 px page on mobile, no hierarchy

Fix: 4-6 key sections on mobile. Hero → Benefits → Social proof → Pricing → CTA.

Where to start?

If you fix only one thing: rewrite your headline (#1). Most-weighted dimension, fastest to change.

Three to fix: headline + CTA above the fold (#2) + demo without gate (#5). Often doubles SaaS landing conversion.
