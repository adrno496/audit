15 critical mistakes on SaaS landing pages (and how to fix them)

The most frequent frictions seen on hundreds of SaaS pages. For each: why it's a problem and the concrete fix.

May 8, 20261,200 words

Before writing this guide, we reviewed 100+ SaaS landing pages — from seed startups to B2B unicorns. The same mistakes recur. Here are the 15 most expensive ones, by frequency.

1. The generic headline that sells nothing

"The next-generation platform to boost your productivity." Could sell a CRM, a project management tool, or a robot vacuum. Nobody remembers it.

Fix: name the job-to-be-done. "Schedule 30 days of Instagram posts in 10 minutes" > "Boost your social media productivity".

2. No CTA above the fold

Visitor arrives, reads the headline, looks for the button… has to scroll to find it. Useless friction.

Fix: a primary CTA visible without scrolling, on desktop and mobile. See our CTA guide.

3. Three competing CTAs of equal visual weight

"Get started", "Watch demo", "Talk to sales" side by side, same color, same size. Visitor freezes.

Fix: one primary CTA (accent color + main size), sober secondary CTAs (text or ghost button).

4. Pricing hidden behind "Request a quote"

In B2B mid-market and SMB, hiding the price is massive friction. 70% of decision-makers want to see the price before booking a call.

Fix: show at least a range or starting point. "From $49/month" beats "On request" a thousand times.

5. Demo that demands email + name + company + phone before showing anything

You ask for 4 fields before the visitor can judge whether your product interests them. Conversion plummets.

Fix: embed a GIF / video / mini interactive demo in the page. The form comes only after value is demonstrated.

6. Blurry, outdated, or unrepresentative screenshots

Screenshot taken in dev mode with fictional data, or worse: a 2-year-old version of the app.

Fix: HD recent screenshots, with realistic (but anonymized) data. Ideally mocked up in a stylized browser frame.

7. Too many customer logos without context

"They trust us: Apple, Google, Microsoft" on a 10-person startup. Nobody believes it.

Fix: if you show logos, make them real and recent. Prefer 4 relevant logos over 12 dubious ones.

8. Stereotypical testimonials without faces

"This product changed my life! — Marie" with a stock photo. Detected from 1 km by any B2B buyer.

Fix: name, role, company, real photo. Ideally with a verifiable LinkedIn link. Read our social proof guide.

9. No price mention on the homepage

Visitor searches "pricing" in the menu, can't find it, scrolls, gives up. Especially on mobile.

Fix: a "Pricing" link in the main menu and a pricing section accessible in under two clicks.

10. Cookie banner that covers everything on mobile

GDPR requires it, but a cookie banner taking 60% of mobile screen with no visible "Reject all" button = frustrated visitor.

Fix: compact banner, clear options ("Accept", "Reject") with equal visual weight.

11. No FAQ or FAQ that doesn't answer real objections

"Is our product great?" instead of "Can I cancel anytime?" and "Do I own my data?".

Fix: list the 5-7 real objections you hear in demos / support. Answer them frankly.

12. Zero security / trust signals on a B2B SaaS

No mention of SOC 2, ISO 27001, GDPR, EU hosting, encryption… while you're targeting companies that care.

Fix: a "Security & compliance" banner with applicable certifications. Crucial for B2B.

13. Hasty competitor comparison

"Us vs Competitor X" table with checkmarks all in our favor. Lack of subtlety = lack of credibility.

Fix: be honest about what the competitor does better than you (one or two points). You gain credibility, and you lower defenses.

14. Auto-play video with sound on the Hero

Visitor lands, computer screams "Hi! Welcome to SaaSXYZ!". They leave before the jingle ends.

Fix: if video, autoplay muted. Sound only on user action.

15. 8,000 px page on mobile with no clear hierarchy

You put everything: features, testimonials, comparison, FAQ, pricing, blog, partners, team, press, podcasts. Visitor scrolls, scrolls, gives up.

Fix: on mobile, aim for 4-6 key sections. Hero → Benefits → Social proof → Pricing → CTA. Everything else is dispensable or goes on dedicated pages.

Recap: where to start?

If you fix only one thing: rewrite your headline (mistake #1). It's the most-weighted dimension and the fastest to change.

If you fix three: headline + CTA above-the-fold (#2) + demo without gate (#5). These three changes alone often double a SaaS landing's conversion rate.
