CTAs that convert: the complete guide

"Learn more" is a dead CTA. Here's how to write, design and place an action button that converts — wording, color, placement, patterns that work.

May 2, 20261,300 words

The anatomy of a converting CTA

  1. Wording — most impactful
  2. Color and contrast
  3. Size and shape
  4. Surrounding micro-copy

Wording: 5 proven rules

1. First-person action verb

"Start my free trial" beats "Start a free trial". The "my" creates ownership.

2. Benefit or outcome, not action

  • "Submit" — neutral, no benefit perceived
  • "Get my quote" — clear benefit
  • "Get my quote in 2 minutes" — benefit + time reassurance

3. Avoid dead CTAs

  • "Learn more" → "See how it works"
  • "Click here" → name the action
  • "Submit" → "Send my request"

4. Address an explicit friction

"Start my free trial · no credit card"
"Audit my site · free, 30 seconds"

5. Stay short (2-5 words)

Past 5 words it becomes a paragraph. Use micro-copy below.

Color: what really matters

The "red converts better than green" myth is false. What matters is contrast. Apply filter: grayscale(1) — the button must stand out.

Size, shape, spacing

Min height: 44 px (Apple HIG). Shape: light rounded corners (8-12 px). Spacing: 24-40 px around.

Placement: where and how many?

2-4 identical primary CTAs spaced through the page. Above the fold, after each major argument, before FAQ, at the bottom.

Micro-copy: what surrounds the button

  • "No credit card required"
  • "Cancel anytime"
  • "14-day money-back guarantee"
  • "No installation required"

Bumps CTR 10-20% in A/B tests.

On mobile

  • Full-width with 16-24 px margin
  • Thumb-position (bottom of screen)
  • Sticky CTA can boost +15-30%

How to test

Test wording first. A/B test 50/50, 2-4 weeks minimum, 95% confidence.
